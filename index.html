Early Life and Education

Dr. Kalam was born in a humble family of boat owners and had to work hard to support his education. He completed his schooling from Schwartz High School in Ramanathapuram and went on to study physics at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli. Later, he pursued aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology, Chennai.

Career

He later served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and also as the Secretary of the Department of Defence Research and Development. In 2002, he was elected as the President of India and served in this position until 2007.

Achievements

Dr. Kalam was a visionary leader and a great scientist. He received numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997. He also received the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan awards in 1981 and 1990, respectively.
